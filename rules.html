Game Rules

Game Setup

Player Configuration

    Small game
    • Two Players:
      • France vs Russia (Europe map)
      • USA vs Japan (Pacific map)
    Large game
    • Two Players:
      • Player 1: Russia + Japan
      • Player 2: USA + France
    • Three Players: Player 3: UK in Africa + Australia
    • Four Players: Like two but each player has its own country.
    • Five Players: Like four and one watches the game.
    • Six Players: Like three but each with its own country.
    • More than Six: Like six and the rest prepares the dinner.
Army setup
    Place an industrial complex in each home territory.
    The initial army of each player is 1 infantry, 1 mechanized infantry, 1 artillery, 1 tank and 50 IPC.

    Start the turn as normal.

Turn

  • All players: Buy army or infrastructure. Place it on the map
  • Each player roll a dice the lowest roll goes first.
    • Declare attacks. See Neutral army in case of attacking neutral territories.
    • Resolve attacks.
    • Quick regroup of up to three units.
    • Time for IPC collection.
    • End turn. Repeat for every player.
  • All players: can regroup units respecting Regrouping rules.
  • Run and resolve Revolts

Game Objective

Goal: Conquer enemies' central territory.

  • Once a central territory is conquered, conquered troops have the following turn to recover it before getting out of the game. At this point the army is considered conquered.
  • In a more than two player game, the player that conquers a central territory rolls a dice:
    • On a 3 or less he preserves the army and infrastructure.
    • On other cases, the troops losing their home territory become neutral territory, and the troops go off the game. Neutral territories rules apply afterwards.
  • The army that conquers a central base wins 50 IPC to spend in amry and infrastructure as player best see fit.

    • Production

    • Each Industrial Complex accounts for 20 IPC of production per turn. All IPCs of production from all complexes is summed up to determine the total production of the player for the turn.
    • Production of units cannot be split across multiple turns.
      • Land units must be located at the Home Base or Military Base.
      • Planes must be located at an Air Base.
      • Ships must be located at a Sea Base.
      • Infantry can be freely placed anywhere in a controlled territory.
    • IPC Collection
      • At end of each player turn, the player chooses one of:
        • Earn 10 IPCs for each of the territories they conquered in the turn.
        • Roll a dice for each territory with land troops, on each 3 or less earn 5 IPCs.
        • Roll two dice, on each 3 or less earn 5 IPCs.
      • Collected IPC accumulate to what already have, to a maximum of 50 IPC.

      Infrastructure

      `
      • Infrastructure requires 2 hits to be destroyed; one hit disables it.
      • Complexes can be attacekd as if they were land units. They always receive one damage if the terrytory is conquered with a combat.
      • In the case of Air bases, if the base is damaged or destroyed the planes must be sent to other bases in the range of movement of the plane or be destroyed.
      • Repairs cost half of production cost.
      • Disabled infrastructure cannot produce, fix, lunch or receive units.
      • Disabled units only can be moved; planes cannot land nor take off from them.
      • Main Industrial Comples:They come with defense against ICBM. Roll three die, on one 3 or less, the missile is destroyed before attack
      • If the missile hits the complex it couts as one hit
      • Disabled main complex can stil collect IPCs
      • Industrial Complex:These does not come with ICBM defense.
      • Military Base:
      • Air Bases: Can hold a max of 5 planes.
      • Naval Bases: Can hold a max of 1 ships docked.
      • ICBM Silo: Can hold 1 ICBM.
      • Roads:Roads allow units to move faster. The first move from road to raad in any movement phase is free.

      Units

      • Any given territory can not have more than 10 units combining all unit types and complexes. Units being transported are not counted.
      • Units being transported cannot defend (excepting planes in not-disabled carriers).
      • If a ship is docked it cannot defend if the territory is attacked, and uniits being transported cannot either.
      • Aircraft Carrier: Can transport up to 3 air units. Requires 2 hits to be destroyed, one hit disables it. Has to dock at a port to be fixed.
      • Transports: Can be by sea or air.
        • Can be used to transport up to two land troops.
        • It cannot attack nor defend from an attack.
        • Air transports can land with troops on an Aircraft Carrier, yet troops must be unloaded at an Air base.
      • AAA: Have an auto defence system that allows them to perform an attack on passing by planes or surface ships at or passing by shore. If an ICBM where to attack a location with an AAA, the latter can do a preentive attack.
      • Submarines: Have an auto defence system that allows them to perform an attack on passing by ships
      • ICBM: Can attack any territory (works like strategic bomber attack).
        • On a 5 or less the attack succeeds.
        • Accounts for any number of hits required to destroy what is on the territory.
        • An AAA can defend from it.
      • Refueler Refueling implies restore the full range for the plane to continue its movement.
      • Can refuel any air unit in the same territory. A plane can be refueled only once per turn. The unit can complete its movement at the refueler in which case in cannot defend if attacked and other planes cannot refuel at the refueler.
  • Regrouping
    • land units, means moving any number of troops follwing own territory up to the maximum movement of the unit type
    • Regrouping air units , moving units to anoter air base or aircraft carrier not passing by any territory with hostile AAA.
    • Regrouping naval units, moving units to nearby empty and without coast sea places or with coast of friendly territory

    Combat

    • A territory can be attacked only once per turn
    • If the territory has no units on it, it is considered conquered as units pass by, this action is counted as an attack on the territory, but units that have more movement can continue their movement and attack another territory.
    • Dice Rolling: Roll one die per unit. Each unit accounts damage according its type.
    • The atack can continue as long as attacking units can damage defending units.
    • If defending units cannot defend against attacking units, the atatack finalizes when the attackers says so, or the die roll makes no damage.
    • The attacker choose what defending unit or complex get the damage.
    • If the territory has constructions, they can also be chosen as a target instead of units.
    • A territory can be attacekd with up to 10 units that can come from different countries. Defending territory defends with all possible units. If the territory is conquered, all attacking units remain in the new territory and complexes or bases gets one damage. Any given unit can only attack an enemy territory with troops once per turn.
    • Land units if able can attack sea units on shore.
    • Attacking neutral territories: Neutral territories can be attacked, The first time a neutral territory is attacked, it rolls a die to determine defending units. Put units according to the roll.
      • 1: no units
      • 2: 1 infantry
      • 3: 1 infantry, 1 mechanized infantry
      • 4: 1 infantry and 1 tank
      • 5: 1 infantry, 1 artillery and 1 tank
      • 6: no units
      • Special rule: in case of rolling 1, 2 or 6, roll again.
    • Revolts Phase where players can invoke a revolt on any enemy (excluding home base) or neutral territory without army for 10IPC.
      • In the same order as the turn, each player can start a revolt in a single territory, to a max of one per turn.
      • If the territroy is owned by the same player and has infrastructure in it, it must be next to a territory owned by the player calling the revolt.
      • The attacking unit is decided in the same way as defending units in Neutral territories applying special rule.
      • If the revolt succeeds, the territory is neutralized. If there is any infrastructure on the territory, it remains undamaged.
      • Attack and defence system works the same as a regular attack.

    Developments (under development ...)

      Every turn an army can spend up to 10 IPC in development. Only one development can be undergoing at a time. A development is complete when the completion dice roll is 5 or 6. A completion dice is rolled every turn after the spend of development IPC reaches 30. An extra completion dice can be bought with 10 IPC.
      • Radar technology:Develop Radar technology. When a radar is located in a territory or aircraft carrier, it allows pre-attack phase at the location with Radar or any other adjacent to it. Artillery gains pre-attack in the same way as AAA.
      • Missile technology:Develop Missile technology. Factories, Air and Sea bases and Aircraft carriers can be defended by Missile technology is a millie luncher is attached. Defends like AAA.
      • ICBM technology:Develop ICBM Launchers and Missiles. ICBM Launchers can hold a max of 2 ICBMs.
      • Paratrooping:Allow infantry to jump in any territory when being transported by planes.
      • Espionage:Allow espionage to be performed. Espionage can be performed on any enevy army. An espionage attempt costs 15 IPC. Roll two dice, if they sum 11 or 12 a successful espionage is performed and any development of that enemy is gained.
      • Land units level up:Land units can be damaged for other units with same or higher level. Before rolling the completion dice, a level up dice must be roll with a value higher that the current level.
      • Air units level up:Air units can be damaged for other units with same or higher level. Before rolling the completion dice, a level up dice must be roll with a value higher that the current level.
      • Sea units level up:Sea units can be damaged for other units with same or higher level. Before rolling the completion dice, a level up dice must be roll with a value higher that the current level.
      • Security level up:Avoid espionage attempts from armies with lower security level. Before rolling the completion dice, a level up dice must be roll with a value higher that the current level.
